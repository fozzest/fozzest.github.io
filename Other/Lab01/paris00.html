About The Wall

Built by Philippe Auguste (Philip Augustus) prior to his leaving on crusade in 1190. Designed to protect against Norman invaders from the north.

Wall was originally 2.5km long. Enclosed an area of 253 hectares.

Colin Jones

"This was power architecture. It made Paris the most heavily – and the most conspicuously – defended military stronghold in western Europe..."

“Building the wall was a way of inhibiting external expansion and stimulating the consolidation of undeveloped land within the walls.”

More Other Locations

The wall can also be viewed in a couple other areas of Paris, notably along Rue Clovis near the Roman-era Arenes de Lutece. This ampitheatre was originally built in the 3rd century CE over the top of one of the first limestone quarries in Paris.

By the time the limestone walls of Philip Augustus were constructed, much of the exposed limestone around Paris was already devoured and used. It was during this time that masons began to gather limestone below ground, constructing tunnels that would one day come to also contain the Catacombs.
